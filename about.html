Why We Built This

Every sewist knows the frustration: you're at the fabric store, holding a bolt, and you're not sure how many yards to buy. Buy too little and you're making another trip. Buy too much and you've wasted money on fabric that sits in a drawer.

I've been sewing for over 12 years — everything from curtains and upholstery for my 1920s bungalow to quilts for friends' babies and the occasional dress for myself. In that time I've made every yardage mistake there is. Enough times that I decided to put the formulas into a tool that anyone can use, for free, without downloading an app or pulling out a calculator.

The math behind our calculator comes from standard industry formulas used by professional upholsterers, curtain workrooms, and pattern makers. We cross-checked our results against real cutting layouts to make sure the numbers hold up on the cutting table, not just on paper.

"The 10% waste allowance isn't random — it comes from actual fabric loss I've tracked across hundreds of projects. Beginners really should use 15%. Trust me, I learned that one the hard way."

— YardageCalc Team

Our Sources & Methodology

Our yardage formulas are based on:

  • ASTM D4964 — Standard test method for fabric width measurement
  • Window Coverings Association of America (WCAA) — Curtain fullness ratio standards
  • Quilting industry standards — Yardage calculations for quilt tops and backing (standard 40" usable width for quilting cotton)
  • Upholstery industry practice — Fabric yardage estimation for standard furniture pieces (sofa, armchair, dining chair)
  • Verified against real cutting layouts from Reader's Digest Complete Guide to Sewing and Vogue Sewing

Each specialized calculator page explains its specific formula and assumptions so you can adjust for your unique project.
