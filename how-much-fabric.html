How Fabric Width Affects Yardage

The same project needs different yardage depending on fabric width. Wider fabric means fewer rows and less total yardage:

Factors That Change Your Yardage

Always buy more than the minimum. You cannot match a dye lot later, and an extra ½ yard is better than being short.
  • Pattern matching (+½–1 yd) — Plaids, stripes, and large prints need extra fabric to align patterns at seams. Each pattern repeat adds up.
  • Shrinkage (+5–10%) — Natural fibers (cotton, linen, wool) can shrink 3–5%. Pre-wash and add waste % accordingly.
  • Nap direction (+¼–½ yd) — Velvet, corduroy, fleece require all pieces cut in the same direction, wasting usable width.
  • Cutting mistakes (+10–15% for beginners) — Inexact cutting, grainline errors, and do-overs happen. Beginners should always add extra.
  • Print placement (+¼–½ yd) — Large motifs (medallions, borders) need centering on each piece, which wastes fabric.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much fabric do I need for a simple dress?
A knee-length simple dress (size M) needs about 2–2½ yards of 45" fabric or 1½–2 yards of 60" fabric. A floor-length dress needs 3–5 yards depending on fullness. Use the calculator above or the detailed clothing yardage guide for exact numbers.
How much fabric for a queen-size quilt?
A queen quilt top (90×96") needs about 6½ yards of 42" quilting cotton if pieced, or 6 yards of 90" wide backing fabric. Binding needs ½–¾ yard. See the quilt calculator for fabric requirements with specific block patterns.
How much fabric for re-upholstering a sofa?
A standard 3-seat sofa needs 10–14 yards of 54" upholstery fabric. Patterned fabrics need 1–2 yards extra for matching. Always ask the upholsterer for their exact estimate before buying. See the upholstery chart for all furniture types.
How do I convert pattern envelope yardage to my fabric width?
Use the calculator at the top of this page. Enter the dimensions of your largest pattern pieces and your actual fabric width. Compare to the pattern table — if the calculator says more, buy the larger amount. See how to read pattern envelopes for detailed instructions.
