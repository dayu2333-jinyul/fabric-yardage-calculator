Napkin Yardage Basics

Cloth napkins are one of my favorite sewing projects — they are useful, eco-friendly, and make great gifts. A dinner napkin is typically 18x18 inches, while lunch napkins are 12x12. The yardage needed is surprisingly small: a set of 8 dinner napkins takes just over a yard of 54-inch fabric.

The key to efficient napkin cutting is maximizing how many fit across your fabric width. On 54-inch fabric, 18-inch napkins need 19 inches per piece (with hem), so 2 fit per width. On 45-inch fabric, 2 also fit since 19 x 2 = 38, well within 45 inches.

Napkin Finishing Options

Napkins can be finished with a simple double-fold hem (my go-to), mitered corners for a professional look, or serged edges for a casual style. Each finish needs slightly different hem allowances. The calculator uses 0.5-inch double-fold hem as standard.
